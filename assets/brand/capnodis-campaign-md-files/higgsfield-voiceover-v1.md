## VOICEOVER PRODUCTION NOTES

### Voice characteristics
- **Language:** Spanish (Spain) — not Latin American
- **Tone:** Calm authority — like an agronomist explaining, not a salesman pitching
- **Speed:** Natural conversational pace, not rushed
- **Energy:** Confident but not pushy; informative, not urgent

### Common words for AI TTS (ElevenLabs / Google TTS / CapCut)
- "gusano cabezudo" — standard Spanish agricultural term
- "19,90 €" — read as "nineteen ninety" (English numerals work better for prices in Spanish ads)
- "calendario" — standard
- "checklist" — English word is fine (Spanish: "lista de verificación" is clunkier)
- "árbol de decisión" — standard Spanish
- "raíces" — standard
- "cuello del árbol" — standard

### Timing adjustments
- If voice runs long → trim last sentence before CTA
- If voice runs short → add breathing pause before final CTA
- Match voice energy to visual mood (Reel 1 = tense, Reel 3 = confident)

### Music cue
- Add soft ambient music UNDER the voiceover
- Music should not compete with voice
- Reel 1: tense/ambient (no vocals)
- Reel 3: clean/professional (short sting at end)
